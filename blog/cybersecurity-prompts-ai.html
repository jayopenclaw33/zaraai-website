How to Use These Cybersecurity AI Prompts

A few ground rules before you start:

  • Never paste sensitive data into public AI tools — substitute realistic placeholders
  • Use Claude or GPT-4 — the larger models produce dramatically better security analysis
  • Customize the context — the more specific you are about your environment, the better the output
  • Always verify — AI can be wrong about technical specifics. Treat output as a starting point, not a final answer

🆓 10 Free Cybersecurity AI Prompts

Threat Intelligence
#1

"You are a senior threat intelligence analyst. I'm going to give you a threat actor description. Analyze it and provide: (1) TTPs mapped to MITRE ATT&CK, (2) likely targets based on historical behavior, (3) detection opportunities at each stage, and (4) top 3 defensive priorities. Threat actor: [paste description]"

Use: Rapid threat actor profiling from open-source reports

#2

"Given the following indicators of compromise (IOCs), generate a structured threat intelligence report suitable for sharing with SOC analysts. Include: context, severity assessment, recommended actions, and YARA/Sigma detection rules where applicable. IOCs: [list your IOCs]"

Use: IOC enrichment and report generation

Incident Response
#3

"You are an incident response lead. I'm sharing initial triage data from a potential security incident. Provide: (1) immediate containment actions, (2) evidence preservation priorities, (3) escalation criteria, (4) next investigation steps, and (5) stakeholder communication template. Incident data: [paste logs/alert summary]"

Use: Incident triage and initial response planning

#4

"Analyze the following Windows Event Log entries and identify: (1) anomalous activity, (2) potential attack techniques, (3) relevant MITRE ATT&CK IDs, and (4) recommended next investigation steps. Present findings in a table format. Log entries: [paste logs]"

Use: Rapid log analysis during active incidents

Vulnerability Management
#5

"You are a vulnerability management specialist. I need you to assess the following CVEs for a [describe environment: cloud/on-prem, OS types, exposed services]. For each CVE, provide: CVSS-adjusted priority for my environment, exploitation likelihood, compensating controls if patching is delayed, and patch/remediation guidance. CVEs: [list CVEs]"

Use: Context-aware CVE prioritization for your specific environment

#6

"Convert the following vulnerability scan results into an executive summary suitable for a non-technical CISO or board presentation. Highlight business risk (not technical severity), prioritized remediation roadmap, and resource requirements. Scan results: [paste summary]"

Use: Translating technical findings into executive-ready communications

Red Team & Penetration Testing
#7

"You are a red team lead planning an adversary simulation exercise. Based on the target profile below, generate a realistic attack scenario including: initial access vector, lateral movement path, target assets, detection evasion considerations, and success criteria. Keep within legal and authorized boundaries. Target profile: [describe environment]"

Use: Red team scenario planning and attack path development

#8

"Draft a penetration testing findings report section for the following vulnerability. Include: vulnerability description, proof of concept summary (no actual exploit code), business impact, CVSS score justification, and specific remediation steps with implementation priority. Finding: [describe finding]"

Use: Accelerating pentest report writing without sacrificing quality

Security Operations
#9

"You are a SOC analyst reviewing a potential phishing email. Analyze the following email headers and body for indicators of phishing. Provide: (1) confidence score (1-10), (2) specific indicators observed, (3) recommended actions for the recipient, and (4) IOCs to add to blocklist. Email: [paste email content/headers]"

Use: Rapid phishing triage and user guidance

#10

"Write a Sigma detection rule for the following attack behavior. Include: rule metadata, detection logic, false positive considerations, and tuning recommendations for high-volume environments. Attack behavior: [describe technique or observable behavior]"

Use: Rapid detection engineering for new threats

Why Prompt Quality Matters So Much in Cybersecurity

The gap between a good AI prompt and a mediocre one is enormous — especially in technical domains like cybersecurity. A vague prompt produces a vague answer. A precisely framed prompt, with the right role, context, and output format specified, produces analysis that actually helps you do your job.

The key elements of a high-quality cybersecurity AI prompt:

  • Role framing: Tell the AI what expert it's playing. "You are a senior threat intelligence analyst" produces better output than no role framing.
  • Context specificity: The more you describe your environment, the more relevant the output.
  • Output format: Specify exactly what you want — table, bullet list, numbered sections, report format. Don't leave it to chance.
  • Constraint awareness: For red team prompts, explicitly scope to authorized activities. This also improves output relevance.
