How to Set Up Your Personal AI Assistant in Under an Hour

A practical, no-fluff guide to getting your own personal AI assistant running today — one that knows who you are, remembers your work, and actually helps you get things done.

✍️ Jax Scott 📅 March 2026 ⏱️ 10 min read

Most people interact with AI as a tool they pick up and put down. They open ChatGPT, ask a question, get an answer, close the tab. Every conversation starts from zero. The AI doesn't know your name, your goals, your work history, or your preferences. It's powerful but impersonal.

A personal AI assistant is different. It knows you. It remembers. It works on your behalf — and it lives on your machine, not in a corporate cloud that resets every conversation.

In this guide, I'll walk you through everything you need to set up your personal AI assistant in under an hour. No coding background required.

What You'll Have at the End

When you finish this guide, you'll have:

  • ✅ A personal AI agent running on your computer
  • ✅ An agent with its own name, personality, and values you define
  • ✅ Persistent memory — it remembers what matters to you across sessions
  • ✅ Access to tools — web search, file reading, and more
  • ✅ The foundation to expand into anything you need

What You'll Need Before Starting

Setup Checklist

  • A computer running Mac, Windows, or Linux
  • Node.js v18+ installed (download here)
  • An API key from Anthropic (Claude) or OpenAI — takes 5 minutes to get, costs cents per conversation
  • About 45–60 minutes of focused time
  • No coding experience required

The Setup Timeline

Here's how the hour breaks down:

1
⏱️ 10 min

Get your API key

Sign up for Anthropic or OpenAI and grab your API key. This is the AI model your assistant will run on. The free tiers are enough to start — a few dollars per month gets you serious usage.

2
⏱️ 10 min

Install OpenClaw

Install Node.js if you haven't already, then run npm install -g openclaw in your terminal. This installs the agent framework that gives your AI persistent memory, tools, and identity.

3
⏱️ 5 min

Create your workspace

Run mkdir my-agent && cd my-agent && openclaw init. This creates the folder structure your agent lives in — its home base where memories and configurations are stored.

4
⏱️ 5 min

Configure your API key

Create a .env file in your workspace with your API key. One line: ANTHROPIC_API_KEY=your-key-here. This connects your agent to the AI model.

5
⏱️ 20 min

Define your assistant's identity

This is the fun part — and the most important. Edit SOUL.md to give your assistant a name, personality, and values. Edit USER.md to tell it about you. The more context you provide, the more personalized everything becomes.

6
⏱️ 5 min

Start your agent and say hello

Run openclaw start and have your first conversation with your personal AI assistant. Give it a task. Watch it respond with the identity and context you defined. You now have a personal AI agent.

Writing Your Identity Files (The Most Important Part)

The identity files are what transform a generic AI into your assistant. Here's how to think about each one:

SOUL.md — Who Your Assistant Is

This defines your assistant's character. Think about:

  • Name — what do you want to call it?
  • Personality — professional and focused? Warm and conversational? Sharp and direct?
  • Values — what principles guide it? What does it prioritize?
  • Communication style — how should it talk to you?

Don't overthink it. Even a few sentences dramatically improve the experience over a generic default.

USER.md — Who You Are

This is where you give your assistant context about your life and work:

  • Your name and what to call you
  • Your profession and what you do
  • Your current projects and goals
  • Your preferences (communication style, tools you use, things to avoid)
  • Any other context that would help a great assistant serve you well

Think of it like briefing a brilliant new assistant on their first day. What would you tell them?

Your First Week: What to Actually Do With It

The setup is just the beginning. Here's how to build the habit of using your personal AI assistant effectively:

Day 1-2: Start with one concrete use case

Don't try to automate your entire life on day one. Pick the most painful, repetitive task in your work — summarizing documents, drafting emails, researching topics — and start there.

Day 3-4: Build your prompt library

Find 3-5 tasks you'll repeat often and create saved prompts for them. For cybersecurity professionals, check out the Cybersecurity AI Prompt Pack. For general workflows, start building your own library.

Day 5-7: Explore the memory system

Ask your assistant to remember important things: "Remember that I prefer bullet points over paragraphs" or "Note that my main project this month is [X]." The more it learns about you, the more useful it becomes.

⚡ The real unlock: Your personal AI assistant gets better the longer you use it. Every session adds to its memory. Every correction shapes its behavior. Unlike a chatbot that forgets everything, your agent compounds — it gets more valuable over time, not less.
